What is Diabetes Mellitus?

Diabetes Mellitus is a long-term medical condition that occurs when the body is unable to properly regulate blood sugar (glucose). This happens either due to insufficient insulin production or the body’s inability to effectively use the insulin it produces.

Primary Types

Type 1

An autoimmune condition where the immune system attacks insulin-producing cells. It usually develops in childhood and requires lifelong insulin therapy.

Type 2

The most common type. It occurs when the body becomes resistant to insulin, often linked to lifestyle factors like diet and inactivity.

Gestational

A temporary condition occurring during pregnancy. It increases future risk for Type 2 diabetes for both mother and child.

Symptoms

  • Frequent urination
  • Excessive thirst
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue & low energy
  • Blurred vision

Risk Factors

  • Genetic predisposition
  • Diet high in sugar/fat
  • Lack of physical activity
  • Obesity or excess weight
  • Hormonal changes
