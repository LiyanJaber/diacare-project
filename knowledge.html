What is Diabetes Mellitus?

Diabetes Mellitus is a long-term medical condition that occurs when the body is unable to properly regulate blood sugar (glucose). This happens either due to insufficient insulin production or the body’s inability to effectively use the insulin it produces.

Primary Types

Type 1

An autoimmune condition where the immune system attacks insulin-producing cells in the pancreas. It usually develops in childhood and requires lifelong insulin therapy.

Type 2

The most common type. It occurs when the body becomes resistant to insulin. It is often linked to lifestyle factors such as diet, inactivity, and weight.

Gestational

A temporary condition occurring during pregnancy. While it usually disappears after birth, it increases future risk for Type 2 diabetes.

Symptoms

  • Frequent urination
  • Excessive thirst
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue & low energy
  • Blurred vision

Risk Factors

  • Genetic predisposition
  • Diet high in sugar/fat
  • Lack of physical activity
  • Obesity or excess weight
  • Hormonal changes

Living with Confidence

Living with diabetes requires responsibility, but it does not prevent you from leading a fulfilling life. Diabetes is not a barrier—it is a condition that requires balance and discipline.

By maintaining healthy habits and following medical advice, you can reduce risks and live independently. You are in control of your journey!
